# Impeccable Design Context — Solennix

## Design Context

### Users
Event organizers and service professionals in LATAM (México, Argentina, and similar markets). They manage weddings, quinceañeras, corporate events, and social gatherings. Primary device: desktop (they're at a desk quoting, planning, and tracking). Mobile is secondary but important for quick lookups. Their job: manage clients, quotes, inventory, payments, and event logistics from one place.

**Emotional goals**: Feel in control of their business. Look professional to their clients. Reduce mental load. Eliminate the chaos of WhatsApp threads, spreadsheets, and paper notes.

**Context of use**: Often managing multiple events in parallel, under pressure, with deadlines. The interface should reduce friction, not add to it.

### Brand Personality
Elegante · profesional · confiable

The tone of a trusted business partner for creative professionals. "I've got you covered" meets "impeccable presentation." Warm and composed — not cold corporate, not startup-casual. Think the confidence of a luxury hotel concierge: efficient, discreet, and always prepared.

References: Honeybook, Dubsado — event professional platforms that balance function with warmth.

### Aesthetic Direction
The existing palette is correct and should be preserved:
- **Primary**: `#C4A265` — warm gold, champagne. Use with intention, not decoration.
- **Accent**: `#1B2A4A` — deep navy. Anchors hierarchy and dark mode surfaces.
- **Light mode**: Warm cream/beige surfaces (`#F5F4F1`, `#FAF9F7`) — inviting, not clinical.
- **Dark mode**: Navy-tinted surfaces (`#0A0F1A`, `#111722`) — rich and composed.

**Direction**: Polish what's there. Refine consistency, improve typography, tighten spacing rhythm. Don't reinvent — elevate.

**Anti-references**:
- No cyan/purple gradients or AI-slop color palettes
- No glassmorphism as decoration
- No generic icon + heading + text card grids repeated endlessly  
- No cold, soulless enterprise SaaS aesthetic
- No startup-playful tone or overuse of emojis/illustrations

### Design Principles

1. **Warmth through precision** — Every pixel is intentional. The warm gold should appear with purpose, not everywhere. Spacing should feel generous and breathable.

2. **Professional without sterile** — The interface serves creatives who care about their presentation. It should inspire confidence, not feel like a spreadsheet or a CRM from 2015.

3. **Information-first hierarchy** — Event data is complex: clients, dates, quotes, payments, inventory. The most important things must be immediately obvious. The squint test must always pass.

4. **Light and dark are equally first-class** — Both modes must feel equally polished and intentional. Dark mode is navy-rich, not generic dark gray. Light mode is warm cream, not pure white.

5. **Typography carries the brand** — System fonts are acceptable but must be used with clear scale, weight contrast, and hierarchy. The size difference between H1 and body should be decisive, not timid.
